Interests

Playing Music

Playing music, electric guitar specifically, crept up on me when I was 17 from a desire to express myself when words wouldn't cut it. For me, words were no match for the natural harmonies and dis-harmonies we can express with music, conveying emotion and feelilng in a more primal way.

Eventually I got into recording on FL Studio. For the most part I just play what I like, and if I can't, I learn the skills required to play it.

Dream Journaling

Many have a different answer for what dreams are and may or may not represent and mean. To me, you can apply the concept of statistics to derive meaning. Suppose a dream is a data point. Alone, meaningless and tells you almost nothing. Chart many points and correlations and themes manifest. Introduce some bias and you have a story. Your story.

Through recording dreams, I've noticed my dream recall down to the details increase significantly. The themes and patterns of the dreams have started to become uniform and predictable. From this I've learned a great deal of insight into how I perceive experiences, my fears and passions, and what changes to make in life.

Philosophy

Applying logic and reason to questions of reality can be enlightening, puzzling, thought provoking and dangerous. But it's a passtime I have done for as long as I can remember. Philosophy to me is taking what we understand to be the self and trying to decipher what it affects, if not everything. Finially trying to answer what is reality without it. Is a given perspective literally reality for that perciever? Are we then creating many realities that we invite or block out other reality-creating individuals? Is homogenous reality our goal, or a world of many colors?

Meditation

It allows you to take bias and preference out of the equation, seeing that thoughts and change in feeling and sensation come and go with or without your intervention (many compare to the tides). Just uh...don't engage in too long a session too many times :)

Programming

Programming for me stems ultimately from laziness, funny enough. If I have to do the same thing many times, why don't I derive a way to automate it? But of course, then I will do the automating many times...so how can I automate the automation? Not to get into a recursive loop, learning to program and infintely abstract is mentally engaging, and many of the concepts we use are derived and related to what we perceive in life. Github's "tree" structure, "objects" possessing "methods" and "properties", abstracting many concepts into one such as "walk" or "speak"...the list goes on. And as I go lower levels down to the CPU instructions, the appreciation I have for those before us only increases.

As much as I like it, I'm very against it at the same time with how much we entrust to machines. It feels often that we are giving a specific set of instructions to handle very impactful and complicated tasks. But of course we make mistakes, and the more impactful the task, the more potential damage and stress involved. So it is both a relaxing and fun hobby as well as a nervewracking one. That's why we keep calm and code on.
